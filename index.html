Welcome to the DRISTI Manual

Disaster Risk, Impact, and Situation Tracking Interface (DRISTI) System

The Disaster Risk, Impact, and Situation Tracking Interface (DRISTI) is an integrated platform combining the strengths of multiple tools, including the Extreme Rainfall Monitoring System (ERMS), Pre-Disaster Risk Assessment (PDRA), Historical Flood and Inundation Maps, and the Climate Explorer.

Tools

ERMS – Extreme Rainfall Monitoring System: Will it trigger a flood?

Using IMERG near real-time data and GFS forecasts, ERMS—built on Google Earth Engine (GEE)—provides early warnings by estimating extreme rainfall locations up to five days in advance. This feature is part of the VAMPIRE hazard monitoring system.
Explore more at: https://bennyistanto.github.io/erm/ or visit VAMPIRE.

PDRA – Pre-Disaster Risk Assessment: Are we exposed? Are we prepared?

This module conducts flood assessments at the Palika level using 2- and 5-year return period flood hazard rasters generated by the Fastflood app. It evaluates vulnerability (e.g., weak infrastructure, unsafe water sources), exposure (e.g., land use, buildings, schools), and capacity (e.g., meteorological stations, health service access, warehouses) in relation to potential flood hazards.

Historical Flood and Inundation Mapping:

Leveraging Sentinel-1 satellite imagery and selected flood timelines, this feature enables rapid pre- and during-flood assessments. It visualizes flood extent and impacts on infrastructure, croplands, and populations both in near real-time and across historical events.
The codebase incorporates contributions from multiple sources, including GEE forums, StackExchange, WFP Indonesia, and UN SPIDER.

Climate Explorer:

Explore the ETCCDI indices for precipitation, anomalies, and more to visualize how climate change is impacting.
